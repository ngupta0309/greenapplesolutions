Small Payments
Need a Change

The cost of cash payments is very high. It includes costs associated with manual acceptance, record keeping, counting, storage, security, transportation and the hassles of finding exact change for small payments.

Mobile money has been growing rapidly across emerging economies and is covering large percentage of the population. Many small merchants also have mobile money accounts and can accept payments using mobile money.

But mobile money services are intrinsically designed for remote transfer and are not very friendly for proximity payments.

Limitations of using mobile money for micropayments

Time required to
make a transaction

Charging patterns where
payer is charged

Need to share phone
number with a stranger

Fragmentation - Only the subscribers of the same MNO can transact with each other

 

Opportunity

 

Millions of small and large merchants around the world are ready to accept cashless payments from billions of consumers who are increasingly signing up for mobile money or other electronic wallets.

In the emerging markets alone, the size of consumer payments is around USD 15 Trillion and is expected to grow to over USD 30 Trillion by 2025.

A large opportunity is waiting for a mobile micropayment system that can leverage mobile wallets for this spend.

An ideal micropayment solution needs to be

Simple to use

Affordable for small merchants and all consumers

Mobile and Portable

Available and accessible

Protective of the user identity

A bold new micropayment solution is all set to change small payments for good.

It's time to welcome the change.
